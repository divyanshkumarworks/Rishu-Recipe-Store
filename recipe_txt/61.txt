Title: Groundnut Chutney Recipe

prep time: 27

Description: Dosa, Idli, Vada and many other Indian foods become tastier and enjoyable with many types of chutneys and Groundnut Chutney is one of the best one. It converts your dull food into sumptuous food and adds healthy punch as it’s prepared using groundnut, chana dal, green chilli and other spices. Follow the below given recipe with step by step photos to make this chutney at home.

Ingredient: Ingredients: 1/2 teaspoon Cumin Seeds (jeera), Salt to taste, 4-5 Curry Leaves, optional, 1/2 cup Roasted Peanuts (skin removed), 1 tablespoon Chana Dal(split chickpeas) or Roasted Chana Dal, 1 small clove of Garlic, optional, 1 Green Chilli (halved), 1 teaspoon Lemon Juice or 1/2 teaspoon Tamarind Paste, 1/3 cup Water (or as needed), 1 teaspoon Oil, 1/4 teaspoon Urad Dal (black lentils without skin), optional, 1/2 teaspoon Mustard Seeds, 3-4 Curry Leaves

Directions:
step-1: Heat 1 teaspoon oil in a pan or a kadai over medium flame. Add 1 tablespoon chana dal.
step-2: Saute until it turns light golden and nice aroma comes. Add 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds, 1 small clove of garlic, 1 green chilli and 4-5 curry leaves.
step-3: Saute for a minute over low flame.
step-4: Remove pan from the flame and let the mixture cool at room temperature. Transfer mixture to the small jar of a mixer grinder.
step-5: Add roasted peanuts, lemon juice and salt.
step-6: Grind until medium coarse texture.
step-7: Add 1/3 cup water (or little less for thick chutney) and grind until smooth. Taste for the salt and lemon juice and add more if required. Transfer it to a bowl.
step-8: Let’s prepare the tempering. Heat 1 teaspoon oil in the same pan over medium flame. Add 1/4 teaspoon urad dal, 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds and 3-4 curry leaves. When urad dal turns light golden brown, turn off the flame.
step-9: Immediately pour the tempering mixture over chutney. Peanut chutney is ready for serving.
